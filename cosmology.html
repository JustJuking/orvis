Orvis is Just the Begining

The realm goes beyond the physical. There are worlds within worlds!

⸻ ✦ ⸻

The universe of Orvis does not spin in perfect, divine harmony. It is a fractured system, bound together by a volatile and unstable Weave. While the scholars of Argenta debate the exact alignment of the celestial spheres, the reality on the ground is far harsher. The boundaries between worlds are dangerously thin—allowing ancient Fae Lords, indifferent celestials, and unspeakable eldritch horrors to bleed into the mortal realm. To understand the cosmos of Orvis is to realize that humanity is merely surviving on a cracked foundation, looking up at a very dangerous sky.

The gods of Orvis are not the benevolent shepherds found in ancient myths; they are distant, unfathomable entities. Whether they are dead, sleeping, or simply indifferent to the struggles of mortals, they rarely intervene directly in the affairs of the world. Worship in Orvis is less about seeking love and guidance, and more about appeasement, survival, and warding off the horrors of the dark. Those who channel their divine power do so with reverence and fear, knowing that drawing the gaze of a god can be fatal.

The cosmology of Orvis builds upon the foundations of DND's longstanding lore. Each of the outer realms has a monarch that rules over the plane. Access to the outer realms is rare and requires powerful magic. The inner planes aremuch more easily accessed, with gateways to each found readily in across Orvis.

Across the Cosmos

The cosmos is divided into several distinct realms, each bound by its own purpose and laws of reality. At the center lie the Inner Planes, where the Material, Astral, and Ethereal planes coexist in a delicate balance. Flanking the Material Plane are its polar reflections: the vibrant Feywild and the desolate Shadowfell. The Inner Planes are completed by the Elemental Planes, which act as primordial wells of raw power that constantly flow into the material world. Beyond all this lie the Outer Planes. Existing outside the bounds of normal reality, these realms are bastions of pure cosmic energy aligned with forces of positive, negative, law, and chaos. Each Outer Plane is governed by a singular entity of near-divine power, though ruthless contenders constantly vie to usurp these thrones.

The Material Plane

The harsh, bleeding center of it all. Home to the frozen wastes of Orvis, the sprawling city of Argenta, and a violently unstable Weave. It is the battleground where mortals fight simply to survive. The land itself seems hostile, battered by brutal winters and stalked by horrors that slink out from the dark.
Civilization here is a fragile construct, maintained by high walls, bitter steel, and the blood of those willing to hold the line. Magic is a desperate tool that routinely turns on its user, and every sunrise is treated as a victory.

"Do not look to the sky for salvation. The gods are deaf, the magic is broken, and the cold will take you if the beasts do not. Keep your blade sharp and trust only the person standing next to you."

— Vaelen the Scarred, Veteran of the Accord

The Astral Plane

The silver void between worlds. It is a haunting, gravity-less graveyard of dead gods, shattered realities, and floating debris where thought dictates movement. Travelers here do not walk; they will themselves forward through an endless, shimmering expanse of twilight.
Scattered throughout this void are the colossal, petrified corpses of forgotten deities and chunks of destroyed worlds. Navigating the Astral Sea requires immense mental fortitude, as the memories and psychic echoes of these dead titans can easily overwhelm a mortal mind.

"I drifted past an eye the size of Argenta. It was stone, yet it wept silver tears. In this place, eternity is not a promise—it is a graveyard."

— Kael, Grand Archivist of the Synod

The Ethereal Plane

The ghostly overlap. A misty, echoing reflection of the Material Plane where unseen watchers, lost souls, and incorporeal predators stalk the living from just beyond the veil. The world here is muffled, drained of color, and shrouded in a cloying fog.
Mortals who slip into the Ethereal Plane can see the Material world as a shadowy outline, but they cannot touch it. It is a terrifying, paranoid existence where you quickly realize how many invisible, hungry things are constantly standing right beside you.

"You think you are alone in your room at night? Step into the mist just once, and you will see the crowd of starving phantoms waiting for your breath to stop."

— Lyana, High Priestess of Azhura

Feywild

A realm of unrestrained, predatory life and overpowering emotion. It is overwhelmingly beautiful, but its Fae Lords are cruel, its bargains are lethal, and time itself is a trap that will steal years from your life.

Shadowfell

A desolate void of decay. It is a monochromatic, freezing wasteland where hope goes to die, shadows hunger for warmth, and the undead reign supreme.

Plane of Earth

A crushing, claustrophobic expanse of shifting stone, jagged crystals, and grinding tectonic plates.

Plane of Air

An endless sky of screaming winds, floating debris, and violent electrical storms that can strip flesh from bone.

Plane of Fire

A raging, choking inferno of magma seas and ash-filled skies. It represents the raw, burning fury of creation and destruction.

Plane of Water

A fathomless, crushing ocean with no surface and no floor, filled with primordial leviathans that hunt in the pitch black.

The Beastlands

A realm of savage, untamed wilderness where the line between hunter and hunted is razor-thin, and civilization holds no power.

Arborea

A place of violent passions and towering, oversized wilderness. The beauty is staggering, but the emotions of its denizens run fatally high.

Ysgard

A rugged, freezing battlefield of floating earth-motes where warriors fight to the death daily, only to resurrect at dawn and do it all over again.

Limbo

A churning soup of pure chaos. Matter constantly shifts, dissolves, and explodes. Casting spells here is a nightmare for the Weave, and surviving it is a test of sheer willpower.

Pandemonium

A pitch-black labyrinth of twisting tunnels, constantly echoing with sanity-shredding winds that drive intruders mad.

Carceri

The ultimate prison. A treacherous string of red-jungle orbs and acidic bogs where escape is nearly impossible and betrayal is the only currency.

The Abyss

A bottomless spiral of chaotic evil. It is comprised of endless, horrific layers ruled by warring Demon Lords who seek only to consume and destroy.

Gehenna

A bleak, volcanic plane of dangerously steep inclines and cruel slopes where nothing is free, mercy does not exist, and survival goes to the most ruthless.

The Grey Waste

A monochromatic realm of pure apathy and spiritual despair. To exist here is to slowly lose your memories, your drive, your sanity, and eventually, your soul.

Acheron

An endless, grinding battlefield of massive iron cubes crashing together in the void, filled with brainwashed armies fighting pointless, eternal wars.

The Nine Hells

A rigid, bureaucratic nightmare of lawful evil. It is ruled by Archdevils who deal in soul-binding contracts, calculated tyranny, and endless, structured torment.

Mechanus

A clockwork universe of turning gears and absolute, emotionless order. Mortals are viewed not as living beings, but as cogs to be calculated, corrected, or filed away.

Arcadia

A realm of militant, regimented harmony. Even the trees grow in perfect geometric rows, and the slightest deviation from the law is swiftly and mercilessly punished.

Bytopia

A dual-layered plane of idyllic labor and wild nature. While outwardly peaceful, the inescapable pressure to conform and produce can slowly unnerve a weary traveler.

Mount Celestiae

A towering, radiant mountain of strict justice and absolute law. To the deeply flawed mortals of Orvis, its blinding perfection is often terrifying.

Elysium

A bastion of absolute, unyielding Good. It is so overwhelmingly peaceful that travelers often lose the will to ever leave, fight, or struggle again.

The Far Realm

The space outside reality. It is an incomprehensible dimension of eldritch horrors, writhing flesh, and non-Euclidean geometry. Even attempting to understand it risks permanent madness.

The Pantheon

The gods of Orvis are aloof, granting power to their followers from afar but almost never intervening directly in mortal affairs. We use the standard Faerûn pantheon as a foundation, alongside several custom deities unique to this setting. Below is a full list of available gods, their alignments, and their domains.

Aethernox, God of Time
Lawful Neutral | Knowledge, Death

The Clockmaker, The Inevitable, The Silver Thread
A cold, unfathomable entity governing the strict flow of time and the finality of death. He is entirely indifferent to mortal struggles, viewing the unstable Weave as a clock running out of sync, waiting to be corrected.

Auril, Goddess of Winter
Neutral Evil | Nature, Tempest

The Frostmaiden, Queen of Air and Darkness, The Cold Breath
A cruel and unforgiving deity who embodies the biting cold of Orvis's harsh winters. She is worshiped mostly out of desperate fear by those hoping to ward off the freezing death she brings.

Azhura, Goddess of Trials
Neutral Good | Nature, War

The Proving Flame, The Crucible of the Worthy, The Unbroken Arrow
A demanding but just goddess who governs rites of passage and the endurance of extreme hardship. She views the brutal world of Orvis as a sacred crucible, rewarding those who face impossible trials and emerge from the fire unbroken.

Azuth, God of Wizards
Lawful Neutral | Nature, Tempest

The High One, The First Magister, Lord of Spells
A strict patron of spellcasters who demands careful study of the volatile Weave. His followers are meticulous scholars trying to prevent magical catastrophes.

Bane, God of Tyranny
Lawful Evil | War

The Black Lord, The Iron Hand, The Dictator
A ruthless god of conquest and absolute control. In Orvis, his followers believe that strict, iron-fisted authority is the only way to survive the chaotic, monster-infested wilds.

Besheba, Goddess of Misfortune
Chaotic Evil | Trickery

The Maid of Misrule, Lady Doom, The Black Antler
A spiteful goddess of bad luck and accidents. Adventurers curse her name when a rope snaps, a weapon shatters, or an unmastered spell backfires violently.

Bhaal, God of Murder
Neutral Evil | Death

The Lord of Murder, The Bleeding Skull, The Silent Blade
A sinister deity of assassination and violent ends. His cults thrive in the shadows, treating murder as a dark sacrament in a world where life is already cheap.

Chauntea, Goddess of Agriculture
Neutral Good | Life

Earthmother, The Grain Goddess, She Who Shapes All
A nurturing but weary goddess of the harvest. In the barren lands of Orvis, her clerics fight desperately to coax life from the frozen, unforgiving soil to keep the settlements fed.

Corthain, God of Civilisation
True Neutral | Knowledge, War

The Builder, The Architect of Walls, The Iron Mason
A pragmatic, grounded god of industry and urbanization. He represents the physical barriers, weapons, and societal structures mortals build to keep the terrifying wilderness at bay.

Cyric, God of Lies
Chaotic Evil | Trickery

The Prince of Lies, The Dark Sun, The Mad God
A chaotic deity of deception, illusion, and paranoia. His influence is often blamed when a survivor's Sanity slips and paranoia takes root in isolated adventuring parties.

Deneir, God of Writing
Neutral Good | Knowledge

The Scribe, Lord of All Glyphs, The First Reader
A quiet god of knowledge and recorded history. His followers act as record-keepers for the Accord, preserving vital survival information that the harsh world constantly tries to erase.

Eldath, Goddess of Peace
Neutral Good | Life, Nature

The Quiet One, Goddess of Singing Waters, Mother of Guardian Groves
A gentle pacifist deity whose clear, hidden waters offer rare moments of respite and Sanity recovery in the brutal wilds of Orvis.

Gond, God of Craft
True Neutral | Knowledge

Wonderbringer, The Inspiration Divine, Lord of Smiths
The pragmatic god of invention and blacksmithing. In a world where a shattered sword means death, Gond's forge-priests are highly valued members of any settlement.

Helm, God of Protection
Lawful Neutral | Life, Light

The Watcher, The Vigilant One, The Unsleeping Eye
A stoic, unyielding god of guardians and protectors. His clerics are often found standing exhausted on the walls of fortified towns, defending against the horrors of the long night.

Ilmater, God of Endurance
Lawful Good | Life

The Crying God, The Broken Lord, The Burden Bearer
A compassionate god who embodies endurance through immense suffering. His faith is widespread among the common folk of Orvis, who endure endless physical and mental hardships.

Kelemvor, God of the Dead
Lawful Neutral | Death

Lord of the Dead, The Judge of Souls, The Great Guide
A stern, impartial judge of the afterlife. He violently hates the undead, and his clerics are dedicated to ensuring the fallen stay resting in the frozen ground.

Lathander, God of Birth and Renewal
Neutral Good | Life, Light

Morninglord, The Rose-and-Gold God, The Dawn Bringer
An optimistic god of dawn and new beginnings. His clerics provide desperately needed light and hope against the encroaching darkness and the horrors of the Shadowfell.

Leira, Goddess of Illusion
Chaotic Neutral | Trickery

Lady of the Mists, Mother of All Illusion, The Veiled Goddess
An enigmatic goddess of mists, deception, and hidden truths. Her followers believe that in a world as deadly as Orvis, a good lie is a much better shield than iron.

Lyra, Goddess of Joy
Chaotic Good | Life

Lady of Revelry, The Joybringer, The Dancer
A bright, rebellious goddess of festivals and happiness. Her followers seek to stave off the crushing despair and Sanity drain of Orvis with music, dance, and revelry.

Loviatar, Goddess of Pain
Lawful Evil | Death

Maiden of Pain, The Willing Whip, The Pale Matron
A cruel goddess who teaches that pain is the only absolute truth. Her cultists are disturbingly resilient, drawing power from their own Exhaustion and gruesome injuries.

Malar, God of the Hunt
Chaotic Evil | Death

Beastlord, The Black-Blooded One, The Tearing Claw
A savage, primal god of predators and the bloodlust of the hunt. He embodies the feral terror of the beasts that stalk the dark woods just outside the city walls.

Mask, God of Thieves
Chaotic Neutral | Trickery

Lord of Aquisitions, Master of Thieves, The Shadowlord
A pragmatic god of stealth, shadows, and thievery. He is favored by those who survive by taking what they need and avoiding direct, lethal confrontation.

Meilikki, Goddess of Forests
Neutral Good | Nature

Lady of the Forest, The Forest Queen, The Ranger's Muse
A distant protector of the untamed woodlands. She guides those who respect the wilds and punishes those who thoughtlessly pillage the natural world.

Molill, God of Poetry and Song
Neutral Good | Light

Lord of Song, The True Voice, Guardian of Singers
A charismatic god of bards, eloquence, and inspiration. His followers preserve the oral histories of Orvis, keeping old legends and survival warnings alive through song.

Myrkul, God of Death
Neutral Evil | Death

Lord of Bones, The Reaper, Old Skull
A terrifying, ancient god of death and decay. He represents the slow, inevitable doom that claims all who wander too far into the freezing wastes.

Mystra, Goddess of Magic
Neutral Good | Knowledge

Lady of Mysteries, The Weave-Weaver, Mother of All Magic
The caretaker of the Weave. In Orvis, her clerics and wizards constantly struggle to mend, understand, and safely channel the violently unstable magic that permeates the world.

Nyxeris, God of Dreams
Chaotic Neutral | Knowledge, Trickery

The Sandman, Weaver of Nightmares, The Whispering Eye
An eldritch, unpredictable deity whose very existence tests the boundaries of mortal Sanity. Followers walk a razor's edge between prophetic enlightenment and permanent madness.

Oghma, God of Knowledge
True Neutral | Knowledge

Lord of Knowledge, Binder of What is Known, The Wise God
A neutral arbiter of facts, secrets, and ideas. His temples are heavily fortified libraries where scholars hoard the blueprints for humanity's survival.

Raven Queen, Goddess of Memories
Lawful Neutral | Death, Knowledge

Lady of the Black Feather, The Watcher, Lexicon of the Slain
An enigmatic, masked deity who governs the exact moment of death, the natural flow of fate, and the hoarding of mortal memories. Her followers view death not with fear, but as a peaceful, inevitable release from the brutal realities of Orvis.

Savras, God of Divination and Fate
Lawful Neutral | Knowledge

The All-Seeing, Lord of Divination, The Third Eye
A cryptic god of destiny and prophecy. Looking into his crystal domain often costs a heavy toll on a mortal's Sanity, revealing futures too grim to bear.

Selune, Goddess of the Moon
Chaotic Good | Knowledge, Life

The Silver Lady, Moonmaiden, The Guide
A guiding light in the darkest nights. She is a goddess of navigation and stars, revered by travelers navigating the treacherous paths between safe havens.

Shar, Goddess of Darkness and Loss
Neutral Evil | Death, Trickery

Mistress of the Night, The Nightsinger, The Lady of Loss
A primeval goddess of pitch-black darkness, forgotten secrets, and bitter grudges. She actively seeks to extinguish the light and drive survivors into absolute despair.

Silvanus, God of the Wild
True Neutral | Nature

The Oak Father, Treebeard, The Ranger
The absolute, uncaring embodiment of wild nature. He represents the brutal reality that the wilderness does not hate mortals; it simply does not care if they survive.

Sune, Goddess of Love and Beauty
Chaotic Good | Life, Light

Firehair, The Lady of Love, Princess of Passion
A goddess of romance, beauty, and passion. In a grim world, her followers fight to protect the fleeting moments of beauty and humanity that make survival worthwhile.

Talos, God of Storms
Chaotic Evil | Tempest

Stormlord, The Destroyer, The Bellowing God
A furious, chaotic god of destruction, lightning, and natural disasters. He embodies the unpredictable, violent weather that ravages the landscape of Orvis.

Thalona, Goddess of Disease and Poison
Chaotic Evil | Death, Nature

Lady of Poison, Mistress of Disease, Mother of All Plagues
A reviled goddess of plagues, rot, and venom. Her cults are treated as immediate, lethal threats to the fragile ecosystems and crowded slums of the cities.

Tempus, God of War
True Neutral | War

Lord of Battles, The Foehammer, The Tactician
An impartial god of conflict and honorable battle. He does not care who wins the war, only that it is fought with bravery and the blood spilled is earned.

Torm, God of Courage and Self-Sacrifice
Lawful Good | War

The True, The Loyal Fury, The Golden Lion
A noble god of duty and self-sacrifice. His paladins often die holding the line against impossible odds so that others might escape the horrors of the wilds.

Tymara, Goddess of Good Fortune
Chaotic Good | Trickery

Lady Luck, The Smiling Lady, Mistress of Chance
A beloved, fickle goddess of luck and gamblers. Adventurers flip coins in her honor right before risking everything on a dangerous Weave roll or a desperate attack.

Tyr, God of Justice
Lawful Good | War

The Even-Handed, The Maimed God, The Blind Lord
A rigid god of law and impartial justice. In a world of fluid morality, his followers strive to uphold absolute, objective laws, even when it is wildly unpopular.

Umberlee, Goddess of the Sea
Chaotic Evil | Tempest

The Bitch Queen, Queen of the Depths, The Wavemother
A malicious, greedy goddess of the oceans and storms. Sailors and coastal dwellers pay her heavy tributes not out of love, but out of absolute, shivering terror.

Valara, Goddess of Rebellion
Chaotic Good | Light, War

The Shattered Chain, The Dawnstriker, Voice of the Voiceless
A fierce, fiery goddess born of desperate uprisings. She empowers those who fight against impossible odds, tyranny, and the crushing weight of Orvis's established powers.

Waukeen, Goddess of Trade
True Neutral | Knowledge, Trickery

The Merchant's Friend, Liberty's Maiden, The Golden Lady
A pragmatic goddess of commerce and wealth. The Accord values her highly, as steady trade and coin are the lifeblood that keeps the city walls repaired and mercenaries fed.
